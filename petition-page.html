Stop the construction of the wall in Big Bend Ranch State Park & Big Bend National Park

The Issue

Big Bend Ranch State Park and Big Bend National Park are among the last truly wild landscapes in the United States. These parks protect over 1,000 square miles of pristine Chihuahuan Desert wilderness — home to endangered species, ancient archaeological sites, and one of the darkest night skies in North America.

The proposed construction of a border wall through this region would devastate one of America's most irreplaceable natural treasures. Wildlife corridors that have existed for millennia would be severed, cutting off migration routes for mountain lions, black bears, javelinas, and countless other species that depend on free movement across the landscape.

The Rio Grande, which forms the natural border through this region, is already one of the most effective natural barriers. Adding a wall would not only be ecologically destructive but would also be redundant — the terrain itself makes unauthorized crossing nearly impossible through most of this area.

Beyond the ecological damage, construction would permanently alter the character of a landscape that draws hundreds of thousands of visitors each year, contributing millions to the local economy. The communities surrounding Big Bend depend on tourism and the preservation of this wild heritage.

We are calling on Senator Ted Cruz, the Department of the Interior, and the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers to:

1. Immediately halt any planning or construction of wall segments through Big Bend Ranch State Park and Big Bend National Park.
2. Conduct a full Environmental Impact Assessment before any further action is taken.
3. Explore alternative border security measures that do not require physical barriers through protected wilderness areas.

The beauty and wildness of Big Bend belong to all Americans and to future generations. Once destroyed, it cannot be rebuilt. Please sign this petition and share it widely to protect this national treasure.
